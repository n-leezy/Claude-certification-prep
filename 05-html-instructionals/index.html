How to study (the 60-second version)

  1. Retrieve, don't re-read. The quizzes and flashcards are the studying — testing yourself beats re-reading (the testing effect).
  2. Space it. Run the flashcard "due" pile every session; let the Leitner boxes resurface what you miss.
  3. Interleave. Mix domains (the quiz "interleaved" mode, the full exams). It feels harder — that's why it works.
  4. Explain it back. Use each domain note's "teach-it-back" checklist (Feynman).
  5. Simulate. Take both timed practice exams near the end; remediate every miss.
